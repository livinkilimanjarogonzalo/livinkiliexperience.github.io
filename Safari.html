4 Days Safari (Tarangire, Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater)

Day 1

Arusha to Tarangire National Park. Distance: 140km (2-3 hours).

After breakfast, the tour begins. We stop at Arusha town for any purchases before heading to Tarangire National Park for a game drive. Tarangire is home to a large elephant population and iconic baobab trees, with a high concentration of wildlife. The Tarangire River, the only source of water during the dry season, attracts herds of elephants, giraffes, zebras, and antelopes. Predators like lions, cheetahs, and leopards take the opportunity to strike. Dinner and overnight at Ngorongoro Farm House.

Day 2

Serengeti National Park. Distance: 145 km (3-4 hours)

After breakfast, we drive to Serengeti National Park, the heart of African wildlife. We'll explore the Seronera area, known for its rich wildlife habitats and the Seronera River, providing water to the region. After a picnic lunch, we continue with the game drive. Dinner and overnight at Serengeti Tented Camp.

Day 3

Serengeti to Ngorongoro Crater. Distance: 145 km (2-3 hours)

Early morning game drive as we head to Ngorongoro Conservation Area. On the way, we stop at Olduvai Gorge, where the "Cradle of Mankind" was discovered by Dr. Louis Leakey. Dinner and overnight at Ngorongoro Rhino Lodge.

Day 4

Ngorongoro Crater to Arusha. Distance: 210 km (3-4 hours)

After breakfast, we descend into the Ngorongoro Crater, home to a stunning ecosystem and a wide variety of wildlife, including the endangered Black Rhino. After a game drive and lunch, we head back to Arusha town.

• Arusha National Park

Welcome to Arusha National Park, a rewarding hiking destination. Located close to Arusha city (Safari City), it is accessible by road from Arusha town, about 32 km away. This park, home to the second-highest mountain in Tanzania, offers an unforgettable experience and serves as a great acclimatization refuge for climbing Mount Kilimanjaro.

The park is rich in wildlife, offering game drives, walking safaris, canoeing, cycling, and horse riding. You can spot elephants, buffaloes, giraffes, warthogs, zebras, and antelopes. Notable sights include Little Serengeti (Serengeti Ndogo) and Buffalo Glade, which are truly breathtaking. Other highlights are the Ngurdoto and Meru Craters, Lake Momella, Longili, and the Tululusia Hill and Waterfalls. Accommodation is available at luxury lodges like Momella Wildlife Lodge and tented camps within the park. Visit the park and experience African culture for a lifetime memory.

• Lake Manyara National Park

Located in the northern part of the Tarangire and Ngorongoro Conservation Area, Lake Manyara National Park is renowned for its diverse ecosystem and wildlife. It is home to tree-climbing lions, pink flamingos, hippos in the soda lake, herds of elephants and buffaloes, zebras, wildebeests, and flocks of birds. This park is ideal for a safari holiday, with accommodation at luxury lodges and tented camps inside the park.

• Tarangire National Park

Tarangire National Park is one of the best parks in the northern circuit, known for its large elephant population and baobab trees. It offers a day or two-day visit, recommended to experience the full beauty of the park. The Tarangire River, located in the middle of the park, provides a source of water during the dry season, attracting large herds of elephants, buffaloes, giraffes, zebras, wildebeests, and antelopes, which in turn draws predators such as lions, leopards, and cheetahs.

The best time to visit is from June to October, which is the dry season and the ideal time to witness the migratory cycle of animals. Accommodation options include luxury lodges and tented camps in the park.

• Ngorongoro Conservation Area

Ngorongoro Conservation Area is one of Africa's natural wonders and a UNESCO World Heritage site, making it one of the most extraordinary destinations in Africa. It is one of the few places where you can see the "Big Five" in a single day, including the endangered Black Rhino. The area is named after the Ngorongoro Crater, which has 600-meter-high walls and spans 260 square kilometers.

The caldera supports both human life and over 25,000 animals. Visitors can also witness the migration of two million wildebeests, zebras, and antelopes to Masai Mara. Additional attractions include Maasai cultural villages and the Olduvai Gorge, where you can see ancient human footprints at the museum. Accommodation is available at luxury lodges and tented camps.

• Serengeti National Park

Serengeti National Park is one of the Seven Natural Wonders of Africa and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is a top destination for wildlife safaris and is famous for the Great Migration, where herds of wildebeests and zebras cross the Mara River to Masai Mara in search of green pastures. The vast plains of the Serengeti are home to the Big Five and over 500 species of birds.

The park's stunning scenery offers a paradise for wildlife photographers. It is also known as the filming location for "The Lion King" movie. The best time to visit is during the dry season from June to October and from January to March. From May to November, the park offers something special for every visitor. Accommodation includes luxury lodges and tented camps within the park.
